This is the Prajna Paramita mantra. Keep it close to you at all times, to remind you of its power, its great magic, its ability to heal all suffering.
Gate, Gate, Paragate, Parasamgate, Bodhi Svaha
Go! Go! Go beyond! Go completely beyond!! To enlightenment.
This mantra is impossible to translate. This is partly because it is in Sanskrit and partly because I believe the full meaning of this mantra is inaccessible to those who have not experienced their Buddha natures. The point of the mantra, even, is not necessarily to deliver precise meaning. It is not a container which you can pry open with tools of logic and absorb into your mental models. It is more of a tool to help trigger a mental state in which you can appreciate non-duality and expand from your self into the universe.

I think this interpretation of the mantra becomes clear when you watch other people recite the sutra. The slow, deliberate chanting, timed to the beat of the wooden block, encourage your mind to enter a trance. The more intimiately you listen to the mantra, the more you let go of your conscious interpretation of the sounds, the more your mind and body will expand into the non-dual interpretation of the universe that is at the heart of Prajna Paramita.

"Let go of the eye, and the whole body-and-mind are nothing but the eye; let go of the ear, and the whole universe is nothing but the ear." -- Zen Master Dogen
