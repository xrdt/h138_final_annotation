Description

以無所得故。菩提薩埵。依般若波羅蜜多故。心無罣礙。無罣礙故。無有恐怖。遠離顛倒夢想。究竟涅槃。
The Buddhist practice is mostly a mental one. Once you have dominated your mind and all its misguided understanding of the world, you will be able to face the world without fear or anger or passion.

If there is nothing to be attained, there is nothing left to do but sit back and contemplate the world. Once you have accepted that as truth, you can rely on non-duality to guide your exploration of and unison with the universe.

One can also consider 罣礙 to mean worry. This definition is suitable for the context of the text, but it is not as effective in delivering the message. To be without worry is not as strong a condition as to be without mental obstruction, or to be able to see clearly. There are those who are confidently wrong.

It seems a bit strange to include the portion regarding confused imagination. My first thought was that confused imagination refers to an incorrect conception of Nirvana. I am thinking of all the elaborate images people have created to represent the Christian heaven. My confusion arises because it seems that those who no longer have obstructions should also no longer be confused. They should be able to see clearly, relying on the Supreme Wisdom. Perhaps the idea is that confused imagination is merely another of the mental obstructions you will watch pass by you as you travel in your boat to the other shore of the Enlightened. Maybe this is my confused imagination manifesting.
